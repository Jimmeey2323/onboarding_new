Studio Operations & Procedures

Professional Appearance

All CSTs are expected to maintain a professional appearance with well-groomed attire, light makeup, and neat hair while wearing the Physique 57 Mumbai uniform. Nail polish should be free of chips, and each CST member is responsible for promoting good health and hygiene. The front desk area is designated as a mobile phone-free zone. Our clients are of utmost importance, and you are the first impression they have of Physique 57 Mumbai.


Front Desk Duties

It is your duty to ensure the front desk remains clean, organized, and presentable. CST staff handle confidential client information, so client forms and outreach materials must be neatly filed and readily available. Pens and stationery should be kept in an orderly manner within the designated professional and luxurious space, as this is where we meet our clients. There should be no food, empty wrappers, or empty mugs cluttering the workspace, as it is meant to uphold a professional and luxurious atmosphere for our clients.

Opening Shift Tasks

Securely lock the cash drawer upon the studio's opening and verify the cash balance, ensuring it matches the closing balance in the petty cash report.

Inspect the front desk, locker rooms, washrooms, pantry area, and studios for any housekeeping issues and promptly notify the housekeeping staff of any necessary work or restocking requirements.

Ensure that mats, blue bands, and balls are correctly placed or folded in the appropriate studio.

Monitor the temperature throughout the facility and activate the air conditioning and TFA systems in the studios 20 minutes before the first class of the day.

Maintain a clean, well-organized boutique area with ample stock.

Ensure that at least one CST team member is always present at the front desk.

Use your biometric access key to enter the studio.

Assist housekeeping staff with studio access, and activate the studio lighting, music system, air conditioning, and position the iPad on its stand if needed.

Review the day's schedule and cross-check attendance records from the previous evening's classes. Ensure clients are accurately marked and address any missed client entries or unpaid classes promptly.

Investigate any unpaid classes, and if not documented in the DSR, report this to your reporting manager immediately.

Verify that towels are correctly and neatly folded and ensure an adequate supply for the day. Notify housekeeping if additional towels are required.

Confirm that you have sufficient change in the cash till to begin the day; if not, request assistance from the on-duty office staff to obtain change before the first member arrives.

Ensure trainers arrive on time for their scheduled classes and activate the 'Emergency Call List' in case of any deviations from the schedule.

Studio Closing Procedure

Fill out the closing template and email the information to the Head of Sales, Sr Operations Manager & COO.

Oversee the housekeeping staff and ensure they have vacuumed the studios to the Physique 57 standard (lawn mower effect), the equipment is set neatly and correctly, and the barres have been disinfected.

Check that they have cleaned mirrors in the entire location, including the locker rooms and boutique; no smudges, dust, or finger marks!

Check that the locker rooms and washrooms are clean, and the amenities are fully stocked; inform housekeeping where necessary.

Tally the cash & CC Slips against actual invoices and share the cash report with Operations Manager & Head of Sales & Client Experiences.

Update the DSR, cash Report, Lead Gen & Boutique Tally Sheets & share it with all concerned.

The boutique should be clean, tidy and fully stocked, check the socks/water bottles and refill where necessary.

Make a note of any apparel/accessories that are out of stock and email the Studio Coordinator with a CC to others.

Ensure housekeeping completes the laundry and refills the towels in reception and the locker rooms.

Check Momence for class discrepancies and rectify any unpaid entries where required.

Ensure that there is no member left behind in the studio premises before you exit the studio. Under no circumstances can you leave before the last member has exited the studio.

Close and lock the studio safely and securely!

Treated Fresh Air System (T.F.A.)

Sick Building Syndrome (SBS), an outcome of poor IAQ (Indoor Air Quality), is the condition of a building in which more than 20% of the occupants suffer from adverse health effects, but with no clinically diagnosable disease present. IAQ refers to the physical, chemical and biological characteristics of air in the indoor environment within an occupied building. IAQ relates to the quality of air that we breathe for almost 80% of our life-the time we spend indoors.

Poor IAQ and SBS reports adverse consequences, other than occupational health problems, as loss of productivity and legal liability.

Productivity loss of around 10% is identified in buildings with poor IAQ.

Legal challenges are on the rise, the onus being on facility owners, design consultants, architects etc.

Pre-conditions incoming fresh air.

Delivers fresh air throughout the year at conditions very near inside conditions.

Helps meet ventilation standards without raising energy cost.

Maintains humidity conditions at no additional cost.

Allows reduction in system capacity by 30 to 65%.

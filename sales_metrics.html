Sales Metrics

Total Sales Revenue

Definition: The total income generated from the sale of goods or services over a specific time period.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (e.g., ₹, $, €).

Insights: Tracks overall performance and business growth. Helps set sales targets and evaluate revenue-generating efforts.

Formula:

Total Sales Revenue = Units Sold × Price Per Unit

Example: If 1,000 classes are sold at ₹500 each, the revenue = 1,000 × ₹500 = ₹5,00,000.

Sales Growth Rate

Definition: Measures the percentage increase (or decrease) in sales over a specific period.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Identifies growth trends and evaluates how strategies impact revenue.

Formula:

Sales Growth Rate = (Current Period Revenue - Previous Period Revenue) / Previous Period Revenue × 100

Example: If revenue increased from ₹5,00,000 to ₹6,00,000:

Growth Rate = (6,00,000 - 5,00,000) / 5,00,000 × 100 = 20%

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Definition: The cost incurred to acquire a new customer.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Measures cost-efficiency of marketing and sales efforts.

Formula:

CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Expenses / Number of New Customers Acquired

Example: If ₹1,00,000 is spent on marketing and 50 customers are acquired:

CAC = ₹1,00,000 / 50 = ₹2,000

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

Definition: The total revenue a business can expect from a single customer over their lifetime.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Identifies how much to invest in acquiring and retaining customers.

Formula:

CLV = Average Purchase Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan

Example: If the average purchase is ₹500, a customer buys monthly (12/year), and the lifespan is 3 years:

CLV = ₹500 × 12 × 3 = ₹18,000

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

Definition: The average revenue generated per customer.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Helps evaluate customer value and identify top-performing customer segments.

Formula:

ARPU = Total Revenue / Number of Customers

Example: If ₹5,00,000 is earned from 200 customers:

ARPU = ₹5,00,000 / 200 = ₹2,500

Sales Conversion Rate

Definition: Percentage of leads that convert into paying customers.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Evaluates sales team performance and lead quality.

Formula:

Conversion Rate = Number of Sales / Number of Leads × 100

Example: If 50 leads generated 10 sales:

Conversion Rate = 10 / 50 × 100 = 20%

Churn Rate

Definition: The percentage of customers who stop doing business with you during a period.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Indicates customer retention and loyalty.

Formula:

Churn Rate = Number of Customers Lost / Total Customers at Start × 100

Example: If 10 customers are lost from 100:

Churn Rate = 10 / 100 × 100 = 10%

Retention Rate

Definition: The percentage of customers retained over a period.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Tracks loyalty and long-term value of the customer base.

Formula:

Retention Rate = (Customers at End - New Customers) / Customers at Start × 100

Example: If you retained 90 out of 100 customers and gained 20 new ones:

Retention Rate = (90 - 20) / 100 × 100 = 70%

Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

Definition: Revenue expected from subscriptions or recurring payments each month.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Tracks predictability and sustainability of revenue.

Formula:

MRR = Average Revenue Per Subscription × Total Subscriptions

Example: If subscriptions cost ₹1,000 each and you have 50 subscribers:

MRR = ₹1,000 × 50 = ₹50,000

Sales Pipeline Value

Definition: The total potential revenue from leads in the sales pipeline.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Identifies revenue opportunities and prioritization of leads.

Formula:

Pipeline Value = Σ (Potential Deal Size × Probability of Closing)

Example: If two deals worth ₹10,000 and ₹20,000 have a 50% and 30% chance of closing:

Pipeline Value = (₹10,000 × 0.5) + (₹20,000 × 0.3) = ₹8,000

Average Deal Size

Definition: The average value of deals closed during a period.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Helps measure upselling success and customer value.

Formula:

Average Deal Size = Total Revenue from Deals / Number of Deals

Example: If 5 deals generated ₹50,000:

Average Deal Size = ₹50,000 / 5 = ₹10,000

Win Rate

Definition: Percentage of deals successfully closed out of total opportunities.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Measures the effectiveness of the sales team.

Formula:

Win Rate = Deals Won / Total Deals × 100

Example: If 10 deals were won out of 50:

Win Rate = 10 / 50 × 100 = 20%

Upsell Rate

Definition: Percentage of customers who purchase upgrades or higher-tier products.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Tracks the success of upselling strategies.

Formula:

Upsell Rate = Number of Upsells / Total Customers × 100

Cross-Sell Rate

Definition: The percentage of customers who purchase additional, complementary products or services.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Helps assess the effectiveness of cross-selling strategies and increases the average transaction value.

Formula:

Cross-Sell Rate = Number of Customers Who Purchased Additional Products / Total Customers × 100

Example: If 20 customers out of 200 purchased an additional service:

Cross-Sell Rate = 20 / 200 × 100 = 10%

Lead-to-Opportunity Conversion Rate

Definition: The percentage of leads that are successfully qualified into sales opportunities.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Tracks how effectively the sales funnel converts early leads into actionable opportunities.

Formula:

Lead-to-Opportunity Conversion Rate = Opportunities Created / Leads Generated × 100

Example: If 50 opportunities were created from 200 leads:

Conversion Rate = 50 / 200 × 100 = 25%

Average Sales Cycle Length

Definition: The average time it takes to close a deal from the first contact with a lead.

Unit of Measurement: Time (days, weeks, or months).

Insights: Evaluates sales efficiency and highlights areas where the process can be streamlined.

Formula:

Average Sales Cycle Length = Total Time for All Closed Deals / Number of Closed Deals

Example: If 5 deals took 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40 days:

Average Sales Cycle Length = (20 + 25 + 30 + 35 + 40) / 5 = 30 days

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

Definition: The direct costs associated with producing or delivering a product/service sold.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Determines profitability per product or service and pricing strategy.

Formula:

COGS = Beginning Inventory + Purchases - Ending Inventory

Example: If starting inventory is ₹50,000, purchases are ₹1,00,000, and ending inventory is ₹30,000:

COGS = ₹50,000 + ₹1,00,000 - ₹30,000 = ₹1,20,000

Gross Profit Margin

Definition: The percentage of revenue remaining after deducting the cost of goods sold.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Indicates how efficiently a company uses resources to produce its products or services.

Formula:

Gross Profit Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100

Example: If revenue is ₹5,00,000 and COGS is ₹1,50,000:

Gross Profit Margin = (₹5,00,000 - ₹1,50,000) / ₹5,00,000 × 100 = 70%

Net Profit Margin

Definition: The percentage of revenue left as profit after all expenses are deducted.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Shows overall profitability and financial health of the business.

Formula:

Net Profit Margin = Net Income / Revenue × 100

Example: If net income is ₹1,00,000 and revenue is ₹5,00,000:

Net Profit Margin = ₹1,00,000 / ₹5,00,000 × 100 = 20%

Revenue Per Employee

Definition: The revenue generated per employee in a specific period.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Measures productivity and efficiency of the workforce.

Formula:

Revenue Per Employee = Total Revenue / Number of Employees

Example: If revenue is ₹50,00,000 and there are 10 employees:

Revenue Per Employee = ₹50,00,000 / 10 = ₹5,00,000

Sales Team Efficiency

Definition: Measures the output of a sales team relative to its input (time and resources).

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Highlights how well the sales team performs with available resources.

Formula:

Efficiency = Revenue Generated by Sales Team / Sales Team Cost × 100

Example: If the team generates ₹10,00,000 and costs ₹2,00,000:

Efficiency = ₹10,00,000 / ₹2,00,000 × 100 = 500%

Percentage of Revenue from New Customers

Definition: The percentage of revenue generated from newly acquired customers in a given period.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Tracks the success of customer acquisition efforts.

Formula:

Percentage of Revenue from New Customers = Revenue from New Customers / Total Revenue × 100

Example: If ₹2,00,000 of ₹5,00,000 revenue comes from new customers:

Percentage = ₹2,00,000 / ₹5,00,000 × 100 = 40%

Average Revenue Per Transaction

Definition: The average amount of revenue generated per sale or transaction.

Unit of Measurement: Currency (₹).

Insights: Helps measure upselling success and monitor transaction trends.

Formula:

Average Revenue Per Transaction = Total Revenue / Number of Transactions

Example: If revenue is ₹5,00,000 and there were 1,000 transactions:

Average Revenue Per Transaction = ₹5,00,000 / 1,000 = ₹500

Discount Ratio

Definition: The percentage of revenue lost due to discounts.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Tracks the impact of discounts on overall revenue and profit margins.

Formula:

Discount Ratio = Discount Amount / Gross Revenue Without Discounts × 100

Example: If ₹10,000 was given in discounts from gross revenue of ₹1,00,000:

Discount Ratio = ₹10,000 / ₹1,00,000 × 100 = 10%

Sales Opportunity-to-Win Ratio

Definition: The percentage of opportunities that result in closed deals.

Unit of Measurement: Percentage (%).

Insights: Measures how well the sales team converts opportunities into customers.

Formula:

Opportunity-to-Win Ratio = Deals Won / Opportunities Created × 100

Example: If 15 deals were won out of 50 opportunities:

Opportunity-to-Win Ratio = 15 / 50 × 100 = 30%

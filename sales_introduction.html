Handling First-Time Visitors

What to Do When a New Member Enters the Studio

  1. Warm Welcome:
    • Smile and greet them by name if known.
    • Offer a quick introduction about yourself and the studio.
  2. Check-In Process:
    • Confirm their trial or appointment details.
    • Provide them with a welcome kit if applicable (brochure, class schedule, etc.).
  3. Studio Tour:
    • Highlight:
      • The barre studio and equipment.
      • Locker facilities and amenities.
      • Instructors’ credentials.
    • End the tour by positioning the barre method as the solution to their fitness goals.
  1. Pre-Class Preparation:
    • Guide them on what to expect.
    • Provide support in selecting the right spot and any necessary equipment.
  2. Post-Class Follow-Up:
    • Ask how they felt after the class.
    • Share next steps and recommend packages based on their feedback.

Fun Fact: Physique 57’s barre method burns up to 500 calories in a single session!

Telephonic Conversations

Questions to Ask During the First Call:

  • "Hi [Name], what got you interested in Physique 57?"
  • "Have you had the chance to try barre or group fitness classes before?"
  • "What’s your main fitness goal right now?"
  • "Are there any specific challenges you’ve faced in your fitness journey?"
  • "How often do you envision working out in a typical week?"
  • "What’s most important to you in a fitness studio—is it the results, the atmosphere, or the schedule flexibility?"
  • "Do you prefer working out solo or in a group environment?"
  • "Would you be open to a trial session to experience the difference for yourself?"

Pro Tip: Over the phone, focus on establishing trust and sparking curiosity about what Physique 57 offers. Keep the tone conversational and warm.

Icebreaker Questions

Use open-ended questions to build rapport and discover client needs:

  • "What inspired you to try Physique 57?"
  • "What’s your fitness journey been like so far?"
  • "What’s one thing you’re hoping to achieve with us?"
  • "Have you tried barre before, or is this your first time?"
  • "How do you typically stay active?"
  • "What’s your favorite way to reward yourself after a great workout?"
  • "What’s one thing you look for in a fitness studio?"
  • "What motivated you to focus on fitness right now?"
  • "Are there specific areas you’d like to improve, such as strength, flexibility, or endurance?"
  • "What kind of workouts have you enjoyed in the past?"
  • "What does your ideal workout routine look like?"
  • "How do you usually track your progress or stay motivated?"
  • "Are there any challenges or barriers that have prevented you from sticking to a routine before?"

Pro Tip: Listen attentively and take mental or written notes to use in your recommendations.

Handling Objections

Common Objections and Responses:

  • "I’m not sure if this is for me."

    Response: "That’s totally understandable! What specific concerns do you have? Let’s address them together."

  • "I’ll think about it."

    Response: "Of course. May I follow up with you in a day or two to answer any questions that come up?"

  • "It’s too expensive."

    Response: "Our programs are designed to provide maximum value by delivering visible results. Let’s explore a package that fits your goals and budget."

Post-Trial Session Questions

Key Questions to Ask to Close the Deal:

  • "How did you feel during the class?"
  • "What did you enjoy the most about today’s session?"
  • "Can you see this becoming part of your fitness routine?"
  • "What would achieving your fitness goals mean to you?"
  • "Do you have any specific goals we can help you work toward?"
  • "How did you find the instructor’s guidance and the class structure?"
  • "Would you like to try a few more classes before committing to a package?"
  • "Were there any moments during the session that particularly stood out to you?"
  • "Is there anything you would like more of in your fitness experience?"
  • "What’s your ideal frequency for workouts?"
  • "Would you prefer focusing on group classes or private sessions?"
  • "Do you feel our studio environment aligns with your fitness needs?"

Pro Tip: Always ask open-ended questions to encourage conversation and uncover concerns or motivations.

Suggesting the Right Package

How to Suggest Better:

  • Align Packages with Goals:
    • If the client wants quick results: Suggest an unlimited package.
    • If they have a busy schedule: Recommend class packs for flexibility.
    • For beginners: Start with smaller packs to build consistency.
  • Highlight Value:
    • Break down the cost per class for packages to show savings.
    • Example: "With this package, each session costs less than a coffee at your favorite café!"
  • Use Testimonials:
    • Share success stories of clients with similar goals.
  • Create Urgency:
    • Example: "This offer is only available until the end of the week."

Navigating the Price Objection

Detailed Steps:

  • Acknowledge Their Concern:

    "I completely understand. Investing in yourself can feel like a big step."

  • Reframe the Value:

    Highlight the benefits they’ll gain, such as improved energy, confidence, and results.

    Example: "Think of it as an investment in your health and happiness."

  • Break Down the Cost:

    Show them how the package fits their budget:

    "This package breaks down to just ₹X per class, which is less than most fitness studios of this caliber."

  • Offer Flexible Options:

    Suggest entry-level packages or payment plans if available.

    Example: "We also have smaller packs that give you the same benefits to start with."

  • Use Comparisons:

    "You’d spend the same amount on dining out twice a month, but this gives you lasting benefits."

  • Trial Close:

    "If we could make this work within your budget, would you be ready to start?"

Service Delivery Cycle

The Ideal Service Delivery Process

  1. Pre-Class Engagement:
    • Confirm bookings and send reminders via email or text.
    • Provide a warm welcome upon arrival.
  2. During Class:
    • Ensure the instructor provides personalized attention.
    • Address any client concerns in real-time.
  3. Post-Class Engagement:
    • Ask for immediate feedback.
    • Suggest relevant packages or upcoming events.
  4. Ongoing Engagement:
    • Schedule regular follow-ups.
    • Celebrate milestones (e.g., "You’ve completed 10 classes!").

Service Recovery

Service recovery is about turning a negative experience into a positive one. Follow these steps:

  1. Acknowledge the Issue:

    Listen empathetically to the client’s concern without interrupting.

  • Apologize Sincerely:

    Example: "I’m truly sorry you had this experience. Let’s make it right."

  • Take Action:

    Offer a solution (e.g., complimentary class, extended validity, or priority booking).

  • Follow Up:

    Check back to ensure the client is satisfied.

    • Pro Tip: Empower your team to resolve minor issues on the spot to avoid escalation.

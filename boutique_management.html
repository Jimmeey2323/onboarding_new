Retail Standards And Compliance

To Be Successful:

  • 1st Ammunition
  • The right product & receiving it at the right time.
  • Refresh merchandise constantly.
  • Exciting evolution of product shipping monthly.

Key Elements and Tools:

  • Know your product.
  • Understand the key elements of each brand.
  • Know your sales goals.
  • Communicate your sales goals.
  • Excite your team.
  • Incentives – motivating, involving and exciting CST’s. Create games and sales competitions. Monthly 50% off employee sale rack.
  • Keeping racks replenished is critical for success. Less is more approach.
  • Designated VM days. Designated number of hanger to a Hanging Rack.
  • Turn shipments around in 24hr period.

Studio Floor Plan Guidelines:

  • Maintaining stocked racks is imperative for success.
  • Simplify whenever possible.
  • Organize merchandise by vendor for clarity.
  • Floor plans detail the placement of Face outs, H Racks, Hot Spots, and Walls by designated numbers.
  • Hot Spots, focal areas that capture attention, should be refreshed every few days.
  • Flag deliveries for efficient placement.
  • Utilize the Retail Guidelines for CST training and effective merchandise presentation.
  • Optimize space in the Front Desk area.

Units Per Transaction (UPT) Insights:

  • UPT measures the average number of items purchased per transaction: Total Items Sold / Total Paying Customers.
  • Higher UPT reflects increased customer purchasing, a powerful tool for boosting sales.
  • Emphasize add-on items, as they're easier to sell than convincing a non-buyer.
  • Focus on best sellers, new products, and socks for effective suggestive selling.
  • Aim for a minimum of 2 units per transaction to maximize sales potential.

Visual Merchandising

At Physique 57 we follow certain standards and best practices to create a powerful guest experience and representation of our brand. Visual Merchandising is an important part of creating that powerful experience as it helps attract, engage, and motivate our customer towards our product.

  • Each Boutique has its own needs and space constraints. Everything we merchandise should be intentional, tell a story and easy to shop.
  • A clean, well-organized space is inviting, keeps product from being damaged and is essential for loss prevention.
  • Maintaining the established vision, requires replenishing/restocking and tending to all items in boutiques.
  • Merchandise is hung as you would read a book- left to right/ metal hooks hang left.
  • Complete size run of each garment is displayed smallest to largest with one in each size. (As items sell, back stock may call for duplicate sizes)
  • Each product must hang at an equal distance apart with no hangers overlapping or forced on rack. (18-25 hangers on small rack/35-50 hangers on a larger rack.)
  • Folded items are stacked and folded in the same direction.
  • Pant Hangers, i.e. Bottoms & Bras hang tags must be clipped to the backside of the garment with the price facing out. (Garment must be pulled taut to avoid sagging/gathering.)
  • Hang Tags must be tucked into the product to eliminate dangling.

Receiving Merchandise

  • Packages must be opened and counted/matched up to shipping invoice. (If shipping invoice does not match physical shipment, the studio director must be notified.)
  • Branded Clothing or any item without a hang tag needs a Physique 57 hang tag when it arrives. They can be attached with the retail gun.
  • Security Tag clothing on the right-side bottom seam including bras. (Except FIDI which will be the left side bottom.)
  • Accessories i.e.: Bags, Underwear, and Shoes should be security tagged with the black accessory security tags.
  • Socks should be barcode labeled on the back side of the tag to be hung in the same direction.
  • Clothing should be barcode labeled over the vendor’s barcode.
  • After counting/security tagging and labeling please leave items in the studio director’s designated area of choice.

Retail Maintenance

  • If replenishing, make sure you check inventory on hand first in Momence instead of searching the cabinets for an item that may not be there.
  • Please keep everything clean and organized for the next person if cabinets are opened.
  • When restocking/cleaning make sure standards are followed. The display should have the shortest sleeve in front and get longer towards the back. If the item has rolled sleeves or sleeves tucked into the pocket, please replicate.
  • Always try to be aware of the best sellers and the 'hotspots' in the studio where the clients shop most often — these hotspots should always be restocked with the best sellers OR with new styles received in that you feel will be strong sellers'. Why? These are easy wins for you and for the client.

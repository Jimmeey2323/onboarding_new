Policies & Brand Guidelines

Class Cancellation Policy

  • All cancellations for Studio classes need to be informed to the team via email, WhatsApp or directly on the app at least 12 hours prior to the Scheduled start time of the reserved class.
  • Privates and Workshops: Reservations can be cancelled online or by telephone with no charge up to 24 hours prior to the beginning of class.
  • If you are added to class from a waitlist, you need to confirm your reservation 3 hours prior to the start of class. For classes that begin at 11 am and earlier, please confirm by 6 pm the day before.
  • If a reservation is not cancelled in accordance with the cancellation policy, that class will be deducted from your Class Package.
  • If a reservation is cancelled early (before 12 hours of the Scheduled Class Start time), the class is credited to your Physique 57 account to use towards future reservations.
  • Single classes, class packages, unlimited packages, special promotions, workshops, and memberships are non-refundable. Unlimited packages and class packages cannot be shared amongst clients. If a reservation is not cancelled in accordance with the cancellation policy, you will be charged for that class or workshop.

Fluent Staff Policy

  • Fluent Employees will be afforded complimentary access to Physique 57 classes & facilities (Locker Room for Ladies & Staff Restroom for Men).
  • Fluent Employees will not have any advanced sign-up privileges.
  • Fluent Employees will be pulled from class if the class is full, and a paying client would like their spot. In this case, all members of staff will have priority, and Fluent Employees will be pulled first.
  • This policy may be amended, altered, or discontinued by the Company without prior notice, at the discretion of the Company. Questions regarding this program should be directed to Mitali Kumar.

Vendor & Partner Discount Policy

  • If vendor/partner is with a member of the press or media, that class for both the vendor/partner as well as the press/media guest will be complimentary as long as that press/media guest is coming to Physique 57 for the first time.
  • If the vendor/partner is not in the class with her/his press/media guest, then the class will only be complimentary for the press/media guest provided it is his/her first Physique 57 class or they have a complimentary influencer package.
  • Vendor/partner will be limited to having 2 complimentary press/media guests per class.
  • If vendor/partner is not accompanied by a press/media guest, they will receive a 20% discount on all packages & memberships.
  • This policy may be amended, altered, or discontinued by the Company without prior notice, at the discretion of the Company. Questions regarding this program should be directed to Mitali Kumar.

Rollover Policy

  • All clients who have unused classes within their expired class packages can be offered a rollover on their unused classes.
  • This is an exception policy and can be used only once per client. Do not offer this upfront or as a selling point to clients. We will need to specify that we are making an exception in this singular case.
  • Balance classes are valid for 1 week from their first class back.
  • There will be no further extensions on balance classes, and this cannot be clubbed with packages that have been frozen.
  • This policy is ONLY applicable on class packages and not on any unlimited memberships or single classes.

Leave Policy

Physique 57 believes employees should be provided opportunities to take some time off to achieve a healthy work-life balance. We recognize family responsibilities and the occasional need to be absent from work to attend to personal or domestic emergencies.

Applicability

This policy is applicable to all the employees of AMP Fitness LLP (“AMP”).

Definitions:

  • “Management” shall mean Mrs. Mallika Parekh and any persons appointed by her specifically for matters in relation to this policy.
  • “Year” shall mean the calendar year beginning on the 1st of January.
  • “Holidays” All employees will be entitled to 2 (two) categories of holidays:

Weekly holidays:

  • Every employee will be given 1 (one) weekly holiday.
  • All the employees need not necessarily be given a weekly holiday on the same day of the week.
  • Any employee may be requested, at the discretion of the Management, to work on his / her weekly holiday.
  • Any employee who has been requested by the Management to work on his / her weekly holiday will be provided a compensatory holiday in lieu of such holiday foregone. Provided that such compensatory holiday shall only be permitted on such day as may be approved by the Management.

Compulsory holidays:

  • Every employee will be entitled to the following 4 (four) compulsory holidays in a year:
  • 26th January;
  • 1st May;
  • 15th August;
  • 2nd October.
  • Additionally, the employees will also be entitled to 4 (four) other festival holidays each year as will be agreed between the Management and each of the employees before the commencement of the year.
  • Any employee may be requested, at the discretion of the Management, to work on a compulsory holiday.
  • Any employee who has been requested by the Management to work on a compulsory holiday will be provided a compensatory holiday in lieu of such compulsory holiday foregone. Provided that such compensatory holiday shall only be permitted on such day as may be approved by the Management.

Casual Leave

  • Casual leave is intended to meet special, emergency or unforeseen circumstances.
  • 2 (two) casual leave entitlements will be credited into each employee’s account at the beginning of every quarter of each year.
  • Each employee will be entitled to a maximum of 8 (eight) casual leaves per year.
  • Save to the limited extent as specified above, casual leave will be non-cumulative and any un-availed casual leave at the end of the year will lapse.
  • Casual leave cannot be combined with any other category of leave or with any holiday, save and except as specifically permitted under this policy or by the Management.
  • Not more than 2 (two) days at a time can be availed as casual leave.
  • The previous permission of the Management is required to be obtained before taking casual leave. When this is not possible, the employee shall inform the Management as soon as may be practicable in writing or orally, either directly or through any other person, of the employee’s absence from work and of the probable duration of such absence.
  • The granting of casual leave will depend upon the exigencies of the business and shall be at the sole discretion of the Management.
  • It is clarified that the Management has the sole discretion to determine whether or not any casual leave taken or sought to be taken by the employee is for a genuine special, emergency or unforeseen circumstance and whether the same ought to instead be categorized as any other type of leave.

Paid Leave

  • Every employee is eligible for 18 days Paid Leave in a calendar year.
  • Those employees who join midway, their Paid Leave will be calculated prorated.
  • Paid Leave not availed of or the balance thereof remaining to the credit of an employee in any calendar year will automatically lapse.
  • An employee on Paid Leave shall be entitled to full wages for the period of leave.
  • Paid Leave will be credited on 1st January of every year.
  • An employee may avail of Paid Leave on grounds of sickness on production of medical certificate, in absence of balance Sick Leave.
  • An employee desiring to avail of Paid Leave will have to give not less than 30 days’ notice of his intention to avail leave, except in urgent cases or unforeseen circumstances.
  • Any un-availed PL will not be encashed.
  • There will be no advance PL granted to employees.
  • In case of genuine case of own wedding or death in the immediate family/paternity, advance PL may be granted to a maximum of 5 days, if employee has completed at least 1 year of service with the organization and has been confirmed, on approval from Head HR. Leave application must be sanctioned in advance, before proceeding on leave.
  • Employees during notice period will not be eligible to avail of Paid Leave.

Discretionary leave:

  • Employees may also apply in writing to the Management for leave (not being casual or earned leave).
  • The granting of such leave will depend upon the exigencies of the business and shall be at the sole discretion of the Management. Any such leave granted will be considered as additional discretionary leave.
  • Nevertheless, the Management may in its sole discretion determine whether such leave is to be categorized as casual leave or earned leave and any such determination by the Management shall be binding on the employee.
  • The Management may in its sole discretion determine whether such discretionary leave will be considered as paid or unpaid leave.
  • Unpaid leave:
  • Any casual leave or earned leave taken with the valid prior approval of the Management will be considered as paid leave.
  • However, any casual leave or earned leave taken without the valid prior approval of the Management or taken validly but unauthorizedly extended by the employee, will be considered as unpaid leave to the extent taken without the valid approval of the Management, unless the Management determines otherwise.
  • Any discretionary leave will be unpaid, unless the Management determines otherwise.
  • In case an employee remain/s on leave without pay on three (3) occasion/s, without permission of the Management; or If an employee proceeds on leave without sanction; or If an employee is on leave without informing his or her manager for more than 3 days, HR shall send a letter to the last known address of the employee, asking him or her to report to office immediately. If no satisfactory response is received, services of such employee shall automatically come to an end, and it will be presumed that the employee has abandoned the employment on her/his own accord.

Sick Leave

  • All employees of the organization are eligible to avail Sick Leave (SL). SL is to be availed strictly for the purposes of any illness.
  • All employees are eligible for 5 days sick leave per calendar year to be calculated on prorated basis.
  • Sick Leave cannot be accumulated.
  • An employee on Sick Leave shall be entitled to full wages for the period of leave.
  • In case an employee takes Sick Leave for more than 3 days, he/she needs to submit a medical certificate given by a Registered Medical Practitioner, defining as clearly as possible the nature and probable duration of the illness. Such application shall be submitted, as far as possible, prior to or simultaneously with the commencement of the period for which leave is applied, or within 3 days upon return from leave.
  • The organization may, at its discretion, secure a second medical opinion by requesting to have the applicant to be medically examined by a doctor recommended by the company as soon as possible.
  • Sick Leave cannot be encashed.
  • SL cannot be combined with CL or vice versa. However, on SL being exhausted, it can be combined with PL or CO subject to specific application and approval on submission of medical certificate.
  • In certain sickness during maternity, SL can be combined with ML on producing appropriate medical certificate & reports.

Leave for COVID-19

  • This section shall only be applicable for employees who have worked for a period of 3 (three) months from their time of employment till the time of infection.
  • Employee presumably or positively diagnosed with COVID-19.
  • Employee needs to provide care for a dependent residing in the house.
  • Employee has presumably encountered an infected person.
  • Special leave will be granted based on medical documentation at the discretion of the management.

Maternity Leave

  • This Section shall only be applicable to a woman employee who has worked for a period of 80 (eighty) days or more in the 12 (twelve) months immediately preceding the date of her expected delivery.
  • A pregnant woman employee can take a maximum of 26 (twenty-six) weeks of maternity leave of which not more than 8 (eight) weeks shall precede the date of her expected delivery.
  • A woman employee who legally adopts a child below the age of 3 (three) months can take maternity leave for a maximum period of 26 (twenty-six) weeks from the date the child is handed over to her.
  • A woman employee who is a commissioning mother can take maternity leave for a maximum period of 26 (twenty-six) weeks from the date the child is handed over to her.
  • In the case of miscarriage or medical termination of pregnancy, the concerned woman employee shall be entitled to leave for a period of 6 (six) weeks immediately following the day of her miscarriage or her medical termination of pregnancy.
  • In case of tubectomy operation, a woman employee shall be entitled to leave for a period of 2 (two) weeks immediately following the day of her tubectomy operation.
  • A woman employee suffering from illness arising out of pregnancy, delivery, premature birth of child, miscarriage, medical termination of pregnancy or tubectomy operation shall be entitled to an additional leave for the duration of her illness, subject to a maximum period of 1 (one) month.
  • Any eligible woman employee who is desirous of applying for maternity leave will require to provide the Management with a written application in the format as provided by the Management.
  • The woman employee shall, on the request of the Management, produce such proof as may be specified by the Management in relation to her pregnancy, delivery, adoption, commissioning, miscarriage, medical termination of pregnancy, tubectomy operation, illness arising out of pregnancy, premature birth of child, etc.

Late Entry Policy

  • Physique 57 enforces a strict policy regarding late entry to sessions. Members will not be permitted entry to a session until after 10 minutes from the scheduled start time.
  • In the event of late arrival, members will unfortunately not be allowed to join the class, and no exceptions will be made under any circumstances.
  • However, members will have the option to reschedule their booking to the following class on the same day. If no subsequent classes are available or if they are of a higher level, the member will not be rescheduled to another day, but will instead the class will be debited from their account.
  • Please note that this policy applies to classes with a duration exceeding 50 minutes. For all express classes, including Sweat in 30, entry is not permitted once the class has commenced, and there is no grace period of 10 minutes.

Class Booking Policy

  • This policy mandates that all members must reserve a spot for their desired class using the official Physique 57 India mobile application, available on the Play Store and the App Store, or by visiting the Physique 57 website.
  • Members are strongly encouraged to pre-book their spots well in advance and refrain from attending classes without prior reservation. Failure to comply may result in denial of entry due to limited availability.
  • Please note that the number of individuals allowed entry to each class is strictly limited to the available barre spots. Therefore, arriving without a prior booking is strongly discouraged.

Cash Handling Policy

This Cash Handling Policy outlines the procedures for managing cash inflow at the studio to ensure transparency, accuracy, and accountability in financial transactions. It is imperative that all staff members adhere to these guidelines to maintain the integrity of cash handling processes.

A. Cash Collection:

  • Daily Cash Envelope/Sleeve: A designated cash envelope/sleeve shall be maintained at the front desk for daily cash transactions.
  • Recording Transactions:
    • The opening shift member is responsible for labeling the envelope/sleeve with the date (DD/MM/YY).
    • Clients shall make payments for packages/memberships or boutique purchases to the Customer Service Team (CST) on duty.
    • CSTs shall provide the necessary change to clients from the float*.
    • After the client departs, CSTs shall place the cash in the envelope/sleeve, noting the amount received along with the client's name under the corresponding date.
  • End-of-Day Tally:
    • The closing CST member is required to reconcile the cash collected with the amount written on the envelope/sleeve and the sales settled in the system.
    • The total collected amount shall be recorded in a Cash Ledger book, which must be maintained accurately.

Petty Cash

All petty cash transactions must receive prior approval from the Operations Manager before proceeding with any purchases.

  • Ordering Process: Contact the designated vendor to place orders for studio supplies. Upon receipt of the ordered items, use petty cash for payment, sign the delivery challan, and file it in the designated folder. All receipts must be retained and stored in the petty cash folder located at the front desk.
  • Documentation: Maintain accurate records by updating the petty cash excel sheet with essential details such as vendor name, ordered products, and total billed amount. Ensure that the excel sheet is formatted for auto-calculation.
  • Accountability: Record the name of the CST staff member responsible for receiving and disbursing the cash for the order.
  • Reconciliation Process: Every three weeks, reconcile the petty cash excel sheet with the receipts to ensure accuracy. Copy and store the receipts in the petty cash folder, along with a printed copy of the excel sheet. Additionally, save a digital copy of the excel sheet to the shared folder at the front desk. Forward the original receipt copies and a copy of the excel sheet to the finance manager for cross-verification. Before submitting the final documentation to the finance department, ensure that the Operations Manager has reviewed, approved, and signed the documents.
  • Discrepancies: In case of any discrepancies, CSTs are obligated to promptly inform their reporting manager.

Cash Deposit

  • Frequency: Cash deposits shall be made daily or every alternate day.
  • Tallying Cash: The total cash in hand must be reconciled with the daily collection recorded in the Ledger Book and the sales reflected in the system (packages/memberships + POS billing). The total collected amount shall be handed over to trusted personnel for depositing in the bank.
  • Bank Deposit Confirmation: Upon completion of the bank deposit, a picture of the Bank Deposit slip along with the corresponding sale amount in the system shall be sent to the Studio Operations Manager. Documentation: A monthly email trail shall be maintained for all Cash Deposits to facilitate reconciliation and verification by the Operations Manager and Accounts team.

Cash Tally

  • Month-End Reconciliation: At the end of each month, the Operations Manager shall aggregate all cash deposit slip amounts. The total amount shall be cross-checked against the corresponding amount in the system. Upon confirmation of accuracy and resolution of any discrepancies, the Operations Manager shall forward the reconciled amount to the Accounts team for further processing.

Adherence to this Cash Handling Policy is essential for maintaining financial integrity and accountability within the studio. Any deviations or concerns regarding cash handling procedures should be promptly reported to the appropriate management personnel for resolution.

Float: A fixed amount of cash kept on hand to provide change for transactions.

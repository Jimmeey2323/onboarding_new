Objection Handling

Types of Objections

Price-Related: Concerns about cost or value.

Time-Related: Perceived lack of time to commit.

Ability-Related: Doubts about fitness level or suitability.

Comparison-Related: Loyalty to other gyms or classes.

Skepticism: Doubts about results or past negative experiences.

Commitment-Related: Hesitation to sign up for long-term packages.

What is an Objection?

An objection in sales is a reason or concern raised by a potential customer that prevents them from committing to a purchase or decision. It’s a natural part of the sales process and often arises from doubts, misunderstandings, or fear of making the wrong decision. Objections can appear at any stage of the sales journey and need to be handled effectively to keep the conversation moving forward.

Handling objections is a key skill for any salesperson. It’s important to recognize that objections provide opportunities to uncover needs and offer solutions, rather than seeing them as obstacles. The way you respond can greatly influence the outcome of the conversation.

Common Types of Objections in Sales

Below are the different types of objections you may encounter when selling memberships/packages at Physique 57 or any other product or service:

“It’s too expensive.”

1. Price Objection

Reason: The customer feels that the price of the product/service doesn’t match the value they believe they will get.

Method to Handle: Reframe the price by focusing on the value of the service. You can also offer alternative payment options, discounts, or highlight the return on investment (ROI).

Tip: Compare the long-term benefits with the cost. Use social proof like testimonials to show that others have benefited greatly.

Response Example:

“I completely understand that the price is a big consideration. What’s important to keep in mind is that you're not just paying for a fitness class—you’re investing in your overall well-being. Physique 57 provides a comprehensive fitness experience that combines strength, cardio, and flexibility in one session, offering you more than just a typical workout. Additionally, with our expert trainers, you’re getting personalized guidance to ensure that you’re making the most out of every class. Many of our members find that the value they receive far exceeds what they might pay elsewhere, especially when you consider the long-term benefits for your health and fitness. If you're worried about the cost, we also offer flexible payment plans to make it more manageable. Would it be helpful if we discussed some options?”

“I found a cheaper or better alternative somewhere else.”

2. Competitor Comparison

Reason: The prospect has compared your offering with a competitor’s and is considering switching because of perceived better value.

Method to Handle: Emphasize what makes Physique 57 unique and how it offers more value than competitors. Focus on the quality of service, expertise of instructors, or unique offerings.

Tip: Differentiate Physique 57 from competitors in terms of the experience, results, and community. Demonstrate exclusivity and highlight features that competitors lack.

Response Example:

“I totally get that there are other options out there, but let me explain what makes Physique 57 stand out. We’re not just another fitness studio. Our method combines barre, cardio, and strength training, which maximizes your results in less time. Plus, our classes are designed with your fitness journey in mind, offering modifications that suit any fitness level. Our instructors aren’t just trainers—they're experts who focus on tailoring the workout to your unique needs, ensuring you get the best results possible. Our members consistently report faster, more sustainable results because of this personalized approach. If you'd like, I can even share a few success stories from people who were in the same position and saw amazing transformations with us.”

“I don’t have time for this.”

3. Lack of Time

Reason: The client feels they are too busy to commit to a fitness routine.

Method to Handle: Reassure the client that the sessions are designed to be efficient, with results in a short amount of time. Show them flexible scheduling options.

Tip: Show efficiency—highlight how effective the workout is in just 57 minutes and how it can fit into their busy schedule. Offer virtual classes or alternative options.

Response Example:

“I understand that finding time in a busy schedule can be a challenge. The beauty of Physique 57 is that our classes are only 57 minutes, which means you get an incredibly effective full-body workout without taking up too much of your day. Plus, you don’t need to spend hours at the gym to see results. We also offer flexible class schedules, and you can easily book a session that fits into your day—whether it’s before work, during lunch, or after work. If even that doesn’t work, we offer virtual classes, so you can take part from the comfort of your own home. We’ve had many members who manage to squeeze in a quick workout, and they say it’s the best part of their day. How does that sound to you?”

“I’m not ready to commit to a long-term membership.”

4. Commitment Issues

Reason: The client is unsure if the program is right for them and doesn’t want to lock themselves into a long-term contract.

Method to Handle: Offer a trial period, short-term memberships, or introductory packages. Show them that they have the option to reassess their decision later.

Tip: Reduce the risk by offering a no-strings-attached trial or flexible terms. Ensure they know they can cancel if it’s not a good fit.

Response Example:

“I completely understand wanting to make sure that this is the right fit for you. That’s why we offer a 10-class package or a one-month membership. It’s a perfect way to get a feel for our classes, the atmosphere, and the results, without committing to a long-term membership upfront. After you’ve had the chance to experience Physique 57 and see how it fits into your life, we can reassess together to see what makes the most sense for your goals. You’ll have no pressure—just the time and flexibility to make the decision that works for you.”

“The studio is too far from my location.”

5. Proximity or Location Objection

Reason: The client finds the location inconvenient and is reluctant to travel long distances.

Method to Handle: Mention other studio locations, virtual classes, or flexible schedules. If applicable, offer a flexible membership that allows them to attend multiple locations.

Tip: Highlight convenience—mention the closest studio or suggest an online option.

Response Example:

“I totally get that traveling can be a challenge, especially with busy schedules. The good news is that we have multiple locations, and I’d love to help you find the one that’s most convenient for you. Additionally, we offer virtual classes, which means you can join from home whenever you need to. No matter where you are, you’ll have access to the same quality classes and results. Would it be easier for you to try a virtual session or visit one of our other studios?”

“I’m worried that the classes might be too intense for me.”

6. Fear of Intensity

Reason: The client may fear they won’t be able to keep up with the class or that it will be too difficult.

Method to Handle: Reassure them that Physique 57’s method is scalable, and classes can be modified to meet all fitness levels.

Tip: Empathy and flexibility—tell them that classes are designed for beginners to advanced levels and that they can go at their own pace.

Response Example:

“It’s completely understandable to feel that way! Physique 57 is designed for every fitness level, from beginners to advanced. The beauty of our program is that the intensity is adjustable. Our instructors are experts in making sure you’re doing the exercises at your pace, with modifications available if needed. You’ll be able to challenge yourself at a level that feels right for you, and as you progress, the exercises can become more challenging—so you’re always improving at your own speed. If you’re ready, I can help you start with a more gradual approach and ensure you’re comfortable every step of the way.”

“I’m not sure I’m ready to start a fitness routine.”

7. Not Feeling Ready

Reason: The client feels they need to be at a certain fitness level before starting or they’re not mentally prepared to make a change.

Method to Handle: Encourage them by focusing on small, manageable steps and how they don’t need to be “ready” to start—just willing to begin.

Tip: Start small—let them know they can ease into the program with lighter classes or modified exercises.

Response Example:

“It’s totally normal to feel uncertain, especially if it’s been a while since you last worked out. But that’s the great thing about Physique 57: it’s a welcoming space for all levels. You don’t need to be ‘ready’—you just need the willingness to start, and we’ll meet you where you are. We’ll guide you through every step and make sure you feel comfortable, even in your first class. Plus, once you start seeing the benefits—whether it’s more energy, improved strength, or better posture—you’ll feel more motivated than ever. We can start with a gentle class that suits you and go from there. How does that sound?”

“I’m not sure if this will work for me.”

8. Skepticism About Results

Reason: The client may have had disappointing results with other fitness programs and is unsure if this one will work for them.

Method to Handle: Share success stories, testimonials, and emphasize the personalized approach. Highlight the results of members who had similar goals or concerns.

Tip: Use social proof—show them how others with similar goals or challenges have found success with Physique 57.

Response Example:

“I totally understand your hesitation. Many of our members had similar concerns before they joined. What sets Physique 57 apart is that our classes are scientifically designed to give you maximum results in the shortest amount of time. Whether you’re looking to tone, strengthen, or lose weight, our method targets all muscle groups and gives you the right balance of cardio and strength training. Plus, we provide regular progress tracking and personalized feedback to ensure you’re always moving toward your goals. We’d love to show you how it works for you—let’s start with a trial class, and you can see the results for yourself!”

“I’m not comfortable in group classes.”

9. Not Comfortable in Group Classes

Reason: The client may feel self-conscious or intimidated in group settings, especially if they’re new to fitness.

Method to Handle: Suggest private sessions or highlight how Physique 57 classes are supportive, non-competitive, and welcoming. Offer reassurance that everyone is focused on personal progress.

Tip: Encourage a comfortable environment—assure them that the group is supportive and welcoming, and that private sessions are an option.

Response Example:

“I hear you, and that’s totally okay. Many of our members felt the same way at first, but they quickly found that our classes are incredibly supportive. Everyone here is focused on their own progress, not comparing themselves to others. Plus, our instructors are experts in making sure you feel comfortable and supported, whether you're in a group or starting with a private session. If you’d prefer, we can start with a one-on-one session to make sure you’re feeling confident, and then you can gradually ease into group classes when you’re ready. Does that sound good to you?”

“I don’t think I need to sign up right now.”

10. No Urgency or Need

Reason: The client doesn’t feel an immediate need for the service or may not understand the urgency of signing up.

Method to Handle: Create urgency by mentioning limited-time offers, special discounts, or membership perks that would make it advantageous to commit now.

Tip: Highlight exclusivity and time-limited offers—let them know that there’s a limited-time promotion or a class filling up.

Response Example:

“I completely understand that you want to be sure you’re ready before making a commitment. However, I wanted to let you know that we’re currently running a [special offer, limited-time promotion, or new member benefit], and I wouldn’t want you to miss out on that. Even if now isn’t the perfect time, we can start by booking a trial class so you can see what the experience is like, and then you can decide if it’s something you’d like to pursue. No pressure at all, just an opportunity to experience it for yourself.”

Important Tips and Tricks for Handling Objections Effectively

  • Always Listen First: Let the customer fully express their concern without interrupting. This shows you care and helps you identify the core issue.
  • Acknowledge Their Concern: Validate the objection by saying something like, “I totally understand where you’re coming from.”
  • Reframe the Objection: Turn the objection into a positive by explaining how it can be a benefit. For example, "I hear that you’re worried about the cost, but investing in your health now can save you money on future medical bills."
  • Use Empathy and Rapport-Building: Make sure they feel heard. “I know it’s tough to commit to something like this, but I promise you’ll see results, and we’ll support you every step of the way.”
  • Present Solutions, Not Problems: Always offer a way forward—whether that’s a different class, a flexible membership, or an introductory offer.
  • Close with a Clear Next Step: Always end by suggesting the next actionable step, whether it’s booking a class, signing up for a trial, or simply learning more about the program.
